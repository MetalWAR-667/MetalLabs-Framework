# 📝 Articles

This section contains essays, reflections and technical write-ups derived from the practical experience of building software with AI-assisted workflows.

Unlike the documentation found in the Framework section, these articles are intentionally more exploratory and discussion-oriented.

They are not specifications.

They are not design documents.

They are opportunities to think out loud, challenge assumptions and document lessons learned along the way.

---

## Purpose

The goal of these articles is to capture ideas that do not naturally belong in technical documentation.

Examples include:

* development workflows;
* engineering observations;
* AI collaboration;
* software architecture reflections;
* project management;
* documentation practices;
* development philosophy;
* lessons learned from real projects.

Some ideas may eventually influence the Framework.

Others may simply remain as historical records of the learning process.

---

## Writing philosophy

These articles do not attempt to establish universal truths.

They represent observations, opinions and conclusions formed within the context of real software development.

Readers are encouraged to question every idea, compare it with their own experience and draw their own conclusions.

---

## Evidence over opinion

Whenever possible, articles should be supported by practical examples, documented experiments or real development experiences.

The objective is not to convince.

The objective is to share observations honestly.

---

## Topics

Typical subjects may include:

* AI-assisted software engineering
* Context engineering
* Documentation workflows
* Architecture and decision making
* Experimental methodologies

* Practical lessons from ongoing projects
