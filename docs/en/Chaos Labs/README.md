# 🧪 Chaos Labs

Chaos Labs is the experimental branch of MetalLabs Framework.

This is where ideas stop being assumptions and start becoming experiments.

Its purpose is not to determine which AI model is "the best".

Its purpose is to observe how different agents behave when they work under the same constraints, with the same information and towards the same objective.

Every experiment is designed to answer a specific question.

Every result should be reproducible.

Every conclusion should be supported by evidence rather than opinion.

---

## Objectives

Chaos Labs investigates questions such as:

* How do different AI agents approach the same engineering problem?
* What kinds of tasks suit each model best?
* How much context is required before useful work can begin?
* How do different prompting strategies influence the outcome?
* What level of human review is required after each proposal?
* Which workflows remain effective regardless of the AI provider?

The objective is not to rank models.

The objective is to improve the development process.

---

## The Chaos Budget

All experiments follow a common protocol called **The Chaos Budget**.

The protocol defines the constraints, mutation limits, evaluation criteria and review process shared by every experiment.

This ensures that results remain comparable even when different AI models participate.

The complete protocol is documented in:

> **CHAOS_BUDGET.md**

---

## Experiments

Each experiment is documented independently.

Typical experiments include:

* behavioural observations;
* workflow evaluations;
* prompt engineering studies;
* context engineering;
* implementation comparisons;
* review cost analysis;
* long-term observations.

Every experiment receives its own document and remains self-contained.

---

## A note on conclusions

Chaos Labs does not attempt to establish universal truths.

Every observation is made within a specific context, using particular tools, projects and constraints.

Readers are encouraged to question the methodology, reproduce the experiments where possible and draw their own conclusions.

---

> *The goal is not to prove who is right.*
>
> *The goal is to understand what can be learned.*
