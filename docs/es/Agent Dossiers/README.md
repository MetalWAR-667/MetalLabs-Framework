# 🤖 Agent Dossiers

Agent Dossiers are long-term observational records maintained throughout the life of the project.

They are not benchmarks.

They are not rankings.

They are not objective measurements of AI capability.

Instead, they document practical observations gathered while collaborating with different AI agents during real software development.

Every dossier evolves over time as new experiments are conducted and additional evidence becomes available.

---

## Purpose

The goal of an Agent Dossier is to accumulate knowledge that cannot be captured by a single experiment.

Examples include:

- recurring strengths;
- recurring weaknesses;
- preferred problem-solving strategies;
- reasoning patterns;
- common failure modes;
- sensitivity to context quality;
- review effort required;
- notable behavioural changes across model versions.

These observations are intended to support future workflow decisions rather than to compare models competitively.

---

## Relationship with Chaos Labs

Chaos Labs produces evidence.

Agent Dossiers accumulate that evidence.

Every completed experiment may contribute new observations to one or more dossiers.

The dossiers themselves should never become the experiment.

They are the long-term memory of the laboratory.

---

## Living documents

AI models evolve continuously.

New versions appear.

Older versions disappear.

Capabilities change over time.

For that reason, Agent Dossiers should be treated as living documents rather than definitive assessments.

Whenever possible, observations should reference the experiment from which they originated.

---

## Interpretation

Every observation recorded here reflects a particular context, project and workflow.

Different developers working under different conditions may reach completely different conclusions.

Readers are encouraged to reproduce experiments whenever possible and form their own opinions.

---

> *Observe first. Conclude later.*
