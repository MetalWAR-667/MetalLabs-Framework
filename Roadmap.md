# 🗺️ MetalLabs Roadmap

**Status:** Living Document

---

# Purpose

This document does not define deadlines or release schedules.

Instead, it describes **how MetalLabs Framework is expected to grow over time.**

The laboratory evolves according to real development needs.

Ideas are not implemented because they sound interesting.

They are implemented because repeated experience demonstrates that they provide value.

---

# Growth Philosophy

MetalLabs grows organically.

Every new document, experiment, workflow or tool should answer a real problem encountered while developing software.

The objective is to avoid unnecessary complexity and allow the laboratory to evolve naturally.

Whenever possible:

> **Need comes first. Structure comes later.**

---

# Growth Stages

## 🌱 Seed

A new idea appears.

At this stage the objective is simply to capture it before it is forgotten.

No implementation is expected.

Possible locations:

* Future notes
* Personal ideas
* Project discussions

---

## 🧪 Experiment

When an idea deserves investigation, it becomes a Chaos Labs experiment.

The goal is to collect observations rather than confirm assumptions.

Not every experiment is expected to succeed.

Negative results are considered valuable knowledge.

---

## 📚 Observation

If multiple experiments reveal recurring patterns, the conclusions may be recorded in:

* Agent Dossiers
* Articles

At this stage the laboratory accumulates knowledge rather than creating methodology.

---

## 📖 Framework

Only ideas that repeatedly prove useful should become part of the Framework.

The Framework documents practices that have demonstrated practical value across real projects.

---

## 🛠️ Tool

A tool should only be created when manual repetition becomes a recurring problem.

Tools are developed to support an existing workflow—not to invent one.

---

# Typical Evolution

A common evolution may look like this:

```text
Idea
    ↓
Seed
    ↓
Experiment
    ↓
Observation
    ↓
Framework
    ↓
Tool
```

Not every idea will complete the entire journey.

Many ideas will remain experiments.

Some will be discarded.

Others may become part of the workflow.

---

# Practical Guidelines

Examples of natural growth include:

### Create a new Agent

When a recurring responsibility appears that is not clearly covered by the current team.

---

### Create a new Context

When a task is repeated often enough that preparing instructions manually becomes unnecessary work.

---

### Create a new Experiment

When an observation deserves evidence before becoming part of the workflow.

---

### Create a new Tool

When repeated manual work can be simplified without changing the workflow itself.

---

### Update the Framework

Only after practical experience consistently demonstrates that a process should change.

---

# About Deadlines

MetalLabs intentionally avoids fixed deadlines.

The laboratory grows alongside real software projects.

Progress is measured by understanding, not by the number of completed documents or tools.

Some ideas may mature in days.

Others may remain unfinished for years.

Both outcomes are considered part of the learning process.

---

# Guiding Principle

> **Do not build for future possibilities.**
>
> **Build for today's recurring problems.**
